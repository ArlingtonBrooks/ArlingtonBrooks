Research Focus

Brooks is a student at the University of Toronto's Aerospace campus. He is a Ph. D. student studying high-order moment methods for modelling non-reactive sprays. His research in high-order moment methods can also be applied to non-equilibrium rarefied gas dynamics.

Research Highlights

  • Brooks is expected to present at the 32nd Rarefied Gas Dynamics conference on the topic "On the Application of Maximum-Entropy Inspired Multi-Gaussian Moment Closure for Multi-Dimensional Non-Equilibrium Gas Kinetics" in July of 2022.
  • Brooks submitted an abstract beside 3 other authors and presented the topic "Eulerian-Based Maximum-Entropy Moment Closures for Modelling Polydisperse Liquid Fuel Sprays" at the 29th annual CFD Society of Canada conference in July of 2021.
  • Brooks presented at the Canadian Aerospace and Space Institute AERO 2021 conference on the topic "Eulerian-Based Maximum-Entropy Moment Closures for Modelling Polydisperse Liquid Fuel Sprays" in June of 2021.
